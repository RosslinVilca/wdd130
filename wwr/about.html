WHITE WATER RAFTING

Rafting is an adventure sport that consists of descending whitewater rivers in a shock-resistant pneumatic raft. Inside each raft or Raft there can normally be between 6 and 8 people accompanied by the guide who is located in the back of the boat, from where he directs it.This adventure tourism activity has had spectacular growth in recent years, being one of the most in demand in Andalusia, Spain and the rest of the world.

History

Rafting comes from the English word raft, which means raft. The origins of this sporting practice date back to the 1950s when the war boats of the Second World War were sought for recreational use in descending rivers. With the development of better materials, the boats were adapted to downhill sports activity. After making a name for itself around the world, rafting began to establish itself as a recreational activity in countries such as the United States and southern and central Europe. The French Alps are where the practice of this discipline achieved greatest success. This is how it ended up reaching our rivers in the Catalan Pyrenees in the early 80s.A group of French people observed the potential that this territory offers for the development of this activity and introduced rafting for the first time in Spain, decades after the raids stopped descending the Noguera Pallaressa.
